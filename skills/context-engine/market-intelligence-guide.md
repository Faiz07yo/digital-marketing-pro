# Market Intelligence Guide

Reference knowledge for macro signal detection, market timing, regulatory tracking, and environmental scanning. Use this to contextualize marketing decisions within broader economic, cultural, and competitive conditions.

---

## 1. Economic Indicators for Marketers

### Leading Indicators (Predict Future Conditions)
- **Consumer Confidence Index (CCI)**: Survey-based measure of consumer optimism. Above 100 = optimistic, below 100 = pessimistic. Leads consumer spending by 2-3 months. Published monthly by the Conference Board
- **Purchasing Managers Index (PMI)**: Above 50 = economic expansion, below 50 = contraction. ISM Manufacturing PMI and ISM Services PMI are both relevant. Services PMI matters more for most B2C marketers
- **Building Permits**: Leading indicator for housing-related industries (home improvement, furniture, real estate marketing)
- **Stock Market Indices**: Wealth effect — rising markets increase consumer willingness to spend on discretionary items. 1-2 month lead on luxury/premium spending
- **Initial Jobless Claims**: Weekly data. Rising claims = deteriorating labor market. 1-2 month lead on consumer spending pullbacks

### Coincident Indicators (Reflect Current Conditions)
- **Retail Sales**: Monthly. Adjust for inflation to see real growth. Break down by category to spot sector-specific trends
- **Personal Income and Spending**: Monthly. When spending grows faster than income, consumers are drawing down savings — unsustainable and signals future pullback
- **Industrial Production**: Relevant for B2B marketers — reflects current manufacturing and business activity levels

### Lagging Indicators (Confirm Trends)
- **Unemployment Rate**: Confirms recession or recovery but lags by months. Still affects discretionary spending and consumer sentiment
- **Personal Savings Rate**: High savings rate = cautious consumers (value messaging needed). Low savings rate = spending confidence (premium messaging viable). Post-COVID savings rate normalization has shifted baseline expectations
- **Consumer Price Index (CPI/Inflation)**: Affects pricing strategy, promotional cadence, and messaging tone. High inflation = emphasize value, stability, and savings

## 2. Economic Conditions and Marketing Response

### Rising Consumer Confidence
- Increase top-of-funnel awareness spending — consumers are receptive to new brands and premium positioning
- Test price increases — willingness to pay rises with confidence
- Launch new products — adoption rates are higher in optimistic environments
- Invest in brand building over performance — consumers are browsing, not just buying necessities

### Falling Consumer Confidence
- Shift to value-oriented messaging — emphasize savings, ROI, practical benefits
- Increase bottom-of-funnel conversion spending — focus on capturing existing demand rather than creating new demand
- Reduce acquisition budgets, protect retention budgets — keeping existing customers is cheaper than acquiring cautious new ones
- Extend promotional periods — consumers need more convincing and take longer to convert
- Avoid tone-deaf luxury/aspirational messaging that ignores financial anxiety

### Inflation Environment
- Anchor messaging on value and price stability ("same price, same quality")
- Highlight bundles, loyalty rewards, and long-term savings
- Adjust promotional cadence — more frequent, smaller discounts over fewer large sales events
- Communicate proactively about any price increases with value justification
- Monitor competitors for price drops that could trigger market share shifts

### Recession Conditions
- Do NOT cut marketing to zero — brands that maintain or increase share-of-voice during recessions gain disproportionate market share in recovery
- Shift budget mix: reduce brand awareness, increase direct response
- Emphasize essential value propositions over aspirational messaging
- Target competitor customers who may be reconsidering their spending
- Prepare recovery-phase campaigns in advance — first to market with optimistic messaging captures early demand

## 3. Cultural Signal Detection

### Social Trend Velocity
Track how fast a topic gains mentions across platforms. The lifecycle of a cultural moment:
1. **Niche emergence** (0-48 hours): Appears in subreddits, Discord servers, niche Twitter/X communities, small TikTok creators
2. **Crossover** (48-96 hours): Picked up by mid-tier creators, cross-posted between platforms, appears in newsletters
3. **Mainstream** (96+ hours): Major media coverage, brand participation begins, memes proliferate
4. **Saturation** (1-2 weeks): Everyone has seen it, engagement drops, participation feels late

**The activation window is during crossover (48-96 hours)**. Earlier is risky (unproven relevance). Later is reactive and feels inauthentic.

### Viral Pattern Recognition
Early indicators that a trend will go mainstream:
- Multi-platform appearance (same topic on TikTok, Twitter/X, and Reddit simultaneously)
- Creator diversity (not just one niche — multiple demographics engaging)
- Emotional resonance (humor, outrage, nostalgia, or shared experience)
- Remix/adaptation pattern (people putting their own spin on the original)
- Accelerating velocity (mentions per hour increasing, not just total mentions)

### Meme Culture Integration
Rules for brand participation in meme culture:
- Only engage if aligned with brand voice (irreverent, playful brands can engage; formal, institutional brands should observe)
- Speed matters more than polish — a rough meme posted in the activation window outperforms a polished version posted late
- Never explain the meme — if your audience does not get it, it is not for your audience
- Avoid political or divisive memes regardless of virality
- One attempt per trend — multiple attempts look desperate

### Cultural Calendar Planning
Plan content around cultural moments that align with brand values and audience identity:
- **Heritage and awareness months**: Plan 4-6 weeks ahead for authentic, substantive content (not performative)
- **Major cultural events**: Super Bowl, Oscars, major holidays — plan 4-8 weeks ahead for paid, 2 weeks for organic
- **Industry events**: Conferences, product launches, regulatory deadlines — plan 2-4 weeks ahead
- **Spontaneous moments**: Keep 10-20% of content calendar flexible for real-time cultural opportunities

## 4. Industry Signal Monitoring

### Competitor Intelligence Signals
- **Funding rounds**: A competitor raising capital signals increased marketing spend in 3-6 months. Prepare defensive campaigns
- **M&A activity**: Consolidation = mature market, possible price increases, integration distractions. Divestitures = strategic shifts, potential market gaps
- **Product launches**: Monitor competitor product pages, app store updates, patent filings, and job postings (hiring for a new product line = launch incoming)
- **Leadership changes**: New CMO typically means agency review (6-12 month disruption), strategy pivot, and budget reallocation. Opportunity to capture share during transition
- **Pricing changes**: Competitor price drops may signal desperation or market repositioning. Price increases may open up value-positioning opportunities
- **Content strategy shifts**: Sudden increase in competitor content output, new topic areas, or new channels = strategic pivot worth monitoring

### Job Posting Analysis
Competitor job postings reveal strategy 3-6 months in advance:
- Hiring for TikTok roles = entering short-form video
- Hiring data scientists = building attribution/personalization
- Hiring enterprise sales = moving upmarket
- Hiring community managers = investing in organic/community growth
- Hiring in new geographies = market expansion

## 5. Platform Algorithm and Policy Signals

### Algorithm Change Detection
- **Sudden reach changes** across multiple accounts (not just yours) = algorithm update
- **Engagement pattern shifts**: If likes increase but shares decrease platform-wide, the algorithm is likely weighting a different engagement signal
- **New format boosting**: Platforms reward early adopters of new features (Reels launch, LinkedIn carousels, Twitter/X Communities). Expect 30-60 days of organic reach boost for new formats
- **Shadowban patterns**: Sudden reach collapse without policy violation notice. Test by posting from a clean account on the same topic

### Policy and Targeting Changes
- **Tracking restrictions**: Apple ATT, Google Privacy Sandbox, platform-specific consent requirements — all reduce targeting precision. Budget implications: broader targeting, more creative variants needed, first-party data becomes more valuable
- **Content moderation shifts**: New restricted categories, stricter enforcement on claims, increased scrutiny on certain industries (health, finance, crypto)
- **API changes**: Deprecations affect reporting, automation, and integration workflows. Monitor developer blogs and changelog pages. Plan migrations 3+ months before deprecation deadlines
- **Ad format changes**: New placements (e.g., AI chat placements), deprecated formats (e.g., expanded text ads), spec changes. Early adoption of new formats often yields lower CPMs

## 6. Regulatory Tracking

### Privacy Law Landscape
| Jurisdiction | Law | Key Requirements | Penalties |
|-------------|------|-----------------|-----------|
| EU/EEA | GDPR | Explicit consent, data minimization, right to erasure, DPO required | Up to 4% global revenue or 20M EUR |
| California | CCPA/CPRA | Opt-out of sale/sharing, right to delete, privacy policy requirements | $2,500-$7,500 per violation |
| Canada | PIPEDA | Meaningful consent, purpose limitation, retention limits | Up to $100K CAD |
| Brazil | LGPD | Consent or legitimate interest, DPO required, data portability | Up to 2% revenue or 50M BRL |
| China | PIPL | Separate consent for cross-border transfers, data localization | Up to 5% annual revenue |

### FTC Guidelines for Marketers
- **Endorsements**: Material connections must be disclosed clearly. "#ad" or "Sponsored" — not buried in hashtags
- **Testimonials**: Must represent typical results or clearly disclose atypicality
- **Comparative advertising**: Must be truthful and substantiated. Cannot cherry-pick metrics
- **Environmental claims**: "Green," "sustainable," "eco-friendly" require substantiation. FTC Green Guides govern specifics
- **AI-generated content**: Emerging guidance — disclose AI use in testimonials and reviews. Do not fabricate reviews

### Industry-Specific Regulations
- **Healthcare (HIPAA)**: Cannot use patient data for marketing without authorization. Tracking pixels on health-related pages may violate HIPAA
- **Financial services (FINRA/SEC)**: All marketing materials must be pre-approved. Performance claims require disclaimers. Social media posts by registered representatives are regulated
- **Alcohol**: Age-gating required. Platform-specific restrictions on targeting. State-by-state shipping laws affect promotion
- **Cannabis**: Federal illegality creates platform advertising bans. State-specific regulations vary dramatically

## 7. Marketing Weather Report Methodology

A single-page weekly brief that synthesizes all signals into actionable guidance.

### Components
1. **Economic conditions** (Sunny/Cloudy/Stormy): CCI trend, retail sales, unemployment direction
2. **Cultural moment opportunities**: Upcoming events, trending topics, seasonal moments
3. **Competitive activity**: New threats, market movements, competitor campaigns
4. **Platform environment**: Algorithm changes, new features, policy updates
5. **Regulatory climate**: New laws, enforcement actions, compliance deadlines

### Overall Marketing Climate Score
- **Green (Favorable)**: Invest aggressively. Consumer confidence up, no regulatory headwinds, platform algorithms stable. Increase budgets, launch new campaigns, test new channels
- **Yellow (Mixed)**: Maintain course with heightened monitoring. Some signals positive, others cautionary. Hold budgets steady, avoid large new commitments, increase measurement rigor
- **Red (Unfavorable)**: Conserve budget and prepare for shift. Economic downturn signals, regulatory crackdowns, platform instability. Reduce experimental spending, focus on proven channels, build contingency plans

## 8. Signal Aggregation and Conviction Framework

### Signal Reliability Hierarchy
1. **Verified economic data** (BLS, Census, Fed): Highest reliability, lowest noise
2. **Platform official announcements**: Reliable but sometimes delayed or incomplete
3. **Multi-source industry reports**: Reliable when 3+ sources agree
4. **Social media trend data**: Moderate reliability, high noise, valuable for speed
5. **Anecdotal reports**: Lowest reliability — never act on a single anecdote

### Conviction Thresholds
- **Single source signal**: Monitor only. Do not change strategy
- **Two confirming sources**: Flag for attention. Begin contingency planning
- **Three or more confirming sources**: Act. Adjust strategy with confidence
- **Contradicting signals**: Investigate the contradiction. Often reveals nuance (e.g., economy strong overall but your industry sector weakening)

### Time Decay
Weight recent signals more heavily than older ones. A signal from this week is worth more than the same signal from last month. Apply exponential decay: weight = 0.9^(weeks_since_signal). Signals older than 12 weeks should be revalidated before acting on them.

### False Positive Management
The cost of acting on a false signal (wasted budget, wrong messaging) must be weighed against the cost of missing a real signal (missed opportunity, competitive disadvantage). For high-cost actions (budget reallocation >20%, campaign pause), require 3+ confirming signals. For low-cost actions (messaging tone adjustment, content calendar update), 2 confirming signals suffice.
